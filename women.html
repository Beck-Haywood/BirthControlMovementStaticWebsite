Leaders of The Birth Control Movement

Margaret Sanger

Born: September 14, 1879

Died: September 6, 1966

Coined the term birth control
Opened the first birth control clinic
She wrote books on birth control leading her to be prosecuted because of the Comstock Act
She founded the planned parenthood federation of america

Mary Dennett

Born: April 4, 1872

Died: July 25, 1947

Wrote a famous pamphlet called “The Sex Side of Life” originally to educate her kids but was distributed later
She was prosecuted because of the Comstock act
She co founded the National Birth Control League
She rallied supporters to fight against laws that made birth control information illegal, like the Comstock Act.

Katharine McCormick

Born: April 4, 1872

Died: July 25, 1947

One of the first women to graduate from Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1904 with a degree in biology
Helped smuggle over 1000 diaphragms from europe into America
Made large orders for diaphramps by posing as a german/french scientist
She established the Neuroendocrine Research Foundation because her husband had schizophrenia and she believed it could have been cured with hormone treatment
She donated 2 million or (20 million today) to help develop the first oral birth control pill
